AI Energy Consumption: How and Where

Visualizing the AI Boom Using HuggingFace's AI Energy Score

By Lucia de la Torre

Artificial intelligence powers everything from chatbots to search engines, but its energy consumption remains largely opaque. While some models require vast amounts of energy to operate on cloud GPUs, others are small enough to run on personal devices. This project explores the environmental impact of AI energy consumption through HuggingFace's AI Energy Score framework.

Energy Consumption Per Task

The chart above illustrates the average energy consumption across ten common AI tasks. Image generation tasks stand out as particularly energy-intensive due to their reliance on large-scale models like Stable Diffusion, while binary text classification consumes minimal energy.

Top 5 Most Energy-Efficient Models

Analysis: The most energy-efficient models include distilgpt2, opt-125m, and gpt2-medium. These models consume minimal GPU energy while maintaining high performance, making them ideal for resource-constrained environments.

Top 5 Least Energy-Efficient Models

Analysis: The least energy-efficient models include phi-4 and Llama-2-13b-hf. These models consume significantly more GPU energy due to their large size and computational intensity.

Public vs Private Models

The charts above highlight differences in energy consumption between public and proprietary models. While open-source models like distilgpt2 are optimized for efficiency, proprietary models often prioritize performance at the cost of higher energy usage. These trade-offs reflect the diverse use cases of AI technology—from lightweight applications to computationally intensive tasks.

Environmental Impacts by Region

The environmental impacts of AI energy consumption vary significantly across regions due to differences in electricity generation methods. Regions with higher Abiotic Depletion Potential (ADPe) values rely more heavily on non-renewable resources like fossil fuels, while Primary Energy (PE) values reflect inefficiencies in electricity generation.

Energy Consumption by Region

The chart above highlights how regional differences in electricity generation influence the environmental impact of AI model deployment. For instance, China’s coal-heavy power mix results in higher Primary Energy values compared to France’s reliance on nuclear power.
